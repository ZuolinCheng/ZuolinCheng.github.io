Hi! I’m Yinxue (Olivia) Wang, an engineer and researcher in machine Learning and bio-image analysis. I got my PhD degree from the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering, Virginia Tech. I’ve been fortunately working with my supervisors Prof. Guoqiang Yu, and Prof. Yue (Joseph) Wang for years, during which I developed skills in various aspects as a machine learning engineer and scholar. Before comming to Virginia Tech, I earned my Bachelor's degree from Tsinghua University, Beijing. My undergraduate thesis was about traffic sign recognition for autonomous vehicles.

My general interests blend machine learning, computer vision, optimization and stochastic signal processing. And my specific research area lies in developing machine learning and computational solutions to the puzzles of extracting meaningful signals from microscopic imaging data. Recently I’m particularly focusing on automatic crowded object (cell) tracking from 3D microscopic videos.

Welcome to my portfolio and thanks for your interest!
