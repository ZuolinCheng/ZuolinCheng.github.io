Hello! Welcome to my portfolio! I am Zuolin Cheng, a bioinformatician with a Ph.D. from Virginia Tech, USA, and a master's from Peking University, China.

My research focuses on bioinformatics, machine learning, optimization, and statistical modeling. I specialize in designing data analysis pipelines for biological research projects, and developing machine learning solutions for extracting meaningful signals from large-scale omics data.

Recently, I've been applying Large Language Models (LLMs) to solve biological problems by leveraging domain knowledge to guide the model towards learning biologically plausible predictions.

I have collaborated with institutions like Stanford University and Cincinnati Children’s Hospital, with my work published in top journals such as Neuron, Cell Stem Cell, and Immunity. Notably, my co-first author paper in Neuron was the best paper of 2019.

CMC: A Machine Learning Model Unifying Various Omics Analysis Tasks

CMC advances various omics analysis tasks by transforming complex tasks into a unified mathematical problem: inferring the effect of multiple hidden factors in large-scale omics data.
[GitHub: CMC] [Paper: CMC]



(CMC Application 1) Driving Transcription Factor(TF) Identification

Driving TF identification aims to pinpoint TFs responsible for biological processes or diseases by mining a large-scale TF-gene binding database. [GitHub: DrivingTF]
Using CMC, we systematically models gene & TF binding affinity heterogeneity, leading to more accurate detection.

We observed significant heterogeneity in gene and TF binding affinities.


The model successfully identified Zfp36l1 as a key regulator of glial fate specification and glioma growth, confirmed by knock-out experiments, leading to genuine biological discoveries.
[Paper: DrivingTF]



(CMC Application 2) scRNA-seq Data Normalization

[GitHub: scRNAseq_Norm]




We observed that “effective cDNA-length” significantly impacts scRNA-seq raw counts.

CMC-based normalization reduces variances of housekeeping genes, while preserving variances of differentially expressed genes (DEGs).



(CMC Application 3) TySim: Quantifying Similarity of Single Cells to Target Cell Type

TySim is the first tool to quantify single-cell similarity to a target cell type (for scRNA-seq data across different sequencing platforms) [Paper: TySim] [GitHub: TySim]

It is developed to address biologists' questions such as:
(1) What is the relationship between a newly discovered cell type and a known cell type?
(2) Does a cell type of interest exist in my single cell population?


TySim enables the identification of cell type that was not discovered due to contamination in scRNA-seq data.


TySim was applied to validate the effectiveness of an inducible genetic tool for tracking microglial subtypes in disease.
[Paper: Immunity]



(CMC Application 4) Gene Ontology (GO) term activity score transformation

A model to transform the scRNA-seq dataset into GO term activity score. [GitHub: GO-Transform]


GO term activity transformation enhances downstream clustering analysis.



(CMC Application 5) Cancer Associated Gene Identification

A model to identify the genes whose mutations are responsible for a particular cancer initiation and progression. [GitHub: CancerGeneFinder]

Question: which gene’s mutations are responsible for cancer’s initiation & progression?

Mutation rates vary across patients, mutation types, and other factors.

CMC reduces potential artefactual findings by modeling the heterogeneity of mutation rates.



GLImpute: Missing Value Imputation by Joint Inference of Global-Local Interaction Network

Missing Value Imputation by joint inference of Global-Local interaction network


GLImpute achieved significantly lower imputation errors regardless of local/global component ratio.


Superior performance of GLImpute on metabolite data
